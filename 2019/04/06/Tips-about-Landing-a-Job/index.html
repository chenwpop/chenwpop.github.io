Tips about Landing a Job

Author: Chen Wahng Date: Apr 06, 2019  13:38:00 Category: Job Hunting

On the second semester of my senior master program, I started to find a full-time job. Unfortunately, I rushed into submitting applications without learning the right way of job hunting, as a result, I wasted 3 months on nothing. At first, Indeed I got several phone interview opportunities since large companies are quite generous at giving out phone interviews on the first month of the hiring cycle, but eventually, I messed up all of them. Now I’m not surprised at this outcome, as I know I wasn’t better than any other candidates at that time. Thanks to my optimism and positiveness, I didn’t give up, and I started to ask people who successfully grab an offer for tips and advice. Most of my friends were very friendly and warm-hearted, and I benefited a lot from them. Finally, after another 2 months of hard working, I got the offer of my current job, a Facebook New Grad Software Engineering, and also 2 similar offers from Amazon and Apple.

Here are some tips I found useful across my job hunting experience. I will make them short and pithy, as more details make them less applicable. Hope they’re helpful to some extends.

Submitting Applications

  • Throw your resume to matched jobs.
  • Be honest on your profile, e.g. gender, ethnicity. If you are an international student, tell them you need H1B sponsorship at the first moment.

Interview

  • Be responsible and highly communicative to your recruiter. Generally speaking, your recruiter can not impact the judgment of your interviewer to you, however, chances are that many candidates are above the standard but interviewers’ schedule is in shortage. In this case, If you contact your recruiter and ask for moving forward to the next step politely, you may get the interview chance while the others are still waiting for recruiter’s response. Moving fast always benefits you when landing a job, after all, every company has an annual head-count limit, first come, first got.
  • Prepare for your interview in the right direction. Read the job qualification carefully to decide what to prepare. If you are still confused about the interview scope, ask your recruiter and they will be happy to help you.
  • The most important thing is to communicate with your interviewer. If you find something is similar to what you have reviewed, do not count your kitchen before they are hatched, calm down and focus on your interviewer’s description. If you are stuck on some point, don’t give up, talk with your interviewer and seek for clues from their answer. Most interviewers are not to frustrate you but to help you.

What If You are Stuck?

  • Remember the most important thing, to communicate with your interviewer.
  • If you are onsite, solve this problem step by step with a simple input on a whiteboard, to activate your brain.
  • First, solve the simpler version. Next, find the difference between the simpler one and the real one, and tackle them.
  • Use an inefficient method first, then optimize it.
  • If you are stuck with a specific point, replace it with a helper() function, and implement it in the final step.

Helpful Sites

  • To learn: I highly recommend 2 algorithm courses by Princeton University on Coursera, Algorithms I, Algorithms II, they are free (at least until I write down this blog), thoroughly illustrate most useful data structures and algorithms, and moderate challenging for me, a new graduate. Most of my interview questions can be solved with the knowledge I learned from them.
  • To practice: The Leetcode or Lintcode, or any other similar website. Make good use of the discussion module, and take notes on amazing ideas either from yourself or others.
  • To show off yourself: The Github and Linkedin. Put all thing that you are proud of on those websites, and mention the link on your resume and job fair talk.
